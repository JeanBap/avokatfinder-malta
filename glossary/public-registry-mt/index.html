Public Registry

The Public Registry is Malta's official archive of all notarial deeds, property titles, and encumbrances, providing the legal foundation for title searches in Maltese property transactions.

Role of the Public Registry

Malta operates two parallel property registration systems: the Public Registry (Registru Pubbliku) and the Land Registry. The Public Registry is the older system — it records notarial deeds chronologically, and title to unregistered property is established by searching back through successive deeds of sale. Most of Malta's older urban property is still in the Public Registry rather than the Land Registry.

How a Title Search Works

A Maltese notary carrying out due diligence for a sale searches the Public Registry for all deeds relating to the property going back at least 30 years (and ideally to the original grant). The search confirms the chain of title, identifies any registered hypothecs (mortgages), judicial annotations (court orders), and servitudes. This is a manual and time-consuming process for older properties with complex histories.

Public Registry vs Land Registry

The Land Registry (established under the Land Registration Act) operates a different system: title is registered by parcel number and a state-guaranteed title certificate (certificate of title) is issued. Once a property is on the Land Registry, title searches are simpler and the registered title is conclusive. New properties and recent subdivisions are increasingly registered in the Land Registry from the outset.

Hypothecs and Annotations

A hypothec (mortgage) granted by a Maltese bank must be registered at the Public Registry or Land Registry to be effective against third parties. The priority of competing hypothecs is determined by date of registration. Buyers should ensure any outstanding hypothecs on the seller's title are discharged at or before the deed date — the notary typically coordinates this as part of the transaction.

Online Access and Modernisation

Malta has progressively moved towards digital access to registry records. Notaries can now search the Public Registry electronically for many purposes. However, very old deeds (pre-computerisation) may still require manual archive inspection. Budget extra time and cost for title searches on historic or rural properties where paper records dominate.

Common Mistake

Watch out: Relying on the seller's verbal assurance that the title is clear. Title searches in the Public Registry can reveal hypothecs and annotations that are invisible to the naked eye.

Expert Tip

Pro tip: Ask your notary specifically how far back they will search and whether any gaps exist — old deeds may be missing and gaps in the chain of title create risk that should be addressed before you commit.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is the Public Registry the same as the Land Registry?

No — they are separate systems with different rules. Your notary will identify which register applies.

Does a registered title guarantee I cannot be dispossessed?

Land Registry title is conclusive evidence of ownership — Public Registry title depends on an unbroken chain of deeds.

How do I find out which registry my property is on?

Your notary can check; alternatively, Malta's online eProperty search gives an initial indication.
