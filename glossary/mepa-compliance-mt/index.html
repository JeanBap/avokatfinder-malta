MEPA Compliance

MEPA compliance refers to adherence with permits and conditions issued under the former Malta Environment and Planning Authority, now replaced by the Planning Authority and ERA, whose decisions still bind existing developments.

From MEPA to PA and ERA

The Malta Environment and Planning Authority (MEPA) was the combined planning and environmental regulator until 2016, when it was split into two separate bodies: the Planning Authority (PA) for planning and development control, and the Environment and Resources Authority (ERA) for environmental regulation. Despite the split, all permits and conditions issued by MEPA remain in force and are now administered by whichever successor agency has jurisdiction over the relevant subject matter.

Why MEPA Compliance Still Matters

Thousands of properties in Malta hold development permits issued under MEPA's tenure. Any conditions attached to those permits — restrictions on use, obligations to plant trees, requirements to submit final completion certificates — are still binding on current and future owners. A buyer who purchases a property without checking MEPA permit conditions may inherit unfulfilled obligations.

Completion Certificates

Maltese planning permits typically require a completion certificate to be obtained from the PA once works are finished, certifying that they were carried out in accordance with the approved plans. Many properties in Malta were never granted completion certificates, either because the owner never applied or because works deviated from the approved plans. Without a completion certificate, a property may face difficulties with certain bank mortgages and may be technically in breach of permit conditions.

Searches and Due Diligence

A thorough property search in Malta should include an online PA portal search showing all permit applications, approvals, refusals, and conditions associated with the property. This search is routine for notaries but buyers should specifically confirm it has been done and ask to see the results. Any outstanding conditions, unresolved objections, or pending appeals should be flagged before exchange.

ERA and Environmental Compliance

For properties near designated areas, in coastal zones, or with potential contamination history, an ERA search may also be warranted. ERA administers the Protection of the Environment Act and can issue clean-up orders binding on landowners regardless of whether they caused the contamination. Environmental liability can transfer with title if not detected at purchase.

Common Mistake

Watch out: Assuming a MEPA permit without a completion certificate is fine because 'nothing has been enforced yet'. Enforcement can happen at any time and is inherited by the buyer.

Expert Tip

Pro tip: Request the completion certificate from the seller as a condition of the promise of sale — if one does not exist, make its obtainment (or a formal sanctioning) a condition precedent to the final deed.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I get a mortgage without a completion certificate?

Some Maltese banks require it; others do not. Ask your bank's specific policy before committing to a purchase that lacks one.

Who applies for the completion certificate — buyer or seller?

Typically the owner/seller, as they have the original contractor relationships. Make it a contractual obligation in the konvenju.

What is an ERA protection zone?

Designated areas (often coastal or rural) where development is heavily restricted and environmental conditions are strictly enforced.
