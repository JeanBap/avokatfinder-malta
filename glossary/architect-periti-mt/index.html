Architect / Periti Malta

In Malta, a 'perit' is the term for a qualified architect and civil engineer who designs buildings, obtains planning permits, and certifies works — an essential professional in any construction or renovation project.

What Is a Perit?

Malta uses the term perit (plural: periti) for professionals who are qualified in both architecture and civil engineering — a combined qualification unique to Malta's educational tradition. A perit can design buildings, prepare planning applications, supervise construction, and issue completion certificates. The Kamra tal-Periti (Chamber of Architects and Civil Engineers) regulates the profession.

When You Need a Perit

A perit is legally required for any works that need a Planning Authority development permit above minor thresholds. In practice, this covers all new construction, significant extensions, structural alterations, and changes of use. The perit stamps and signs the application drawings, taking professional responsibility for their accuracy and compliance with the Local Plan.

Perit as Project Manager

Beyond design and permits, many periti in Malta also act as project managers during construction — liaising with contractors, certifying payment milestones, and issuing the final completion certificate. For buyers of off-plan or renovation projects, the perit is the key professional protecting their interests on site.

Finding a Perit

The Kamra tal-Periti maintains a register of licensed periti searchable online. When choosing, consider their experience with your property type (residential vs commercial), their knowledge of the specific Local Plan rules for your area, and their language ability if you are not a Maltese speaker. Always verify their licence is current before instructing.

Fees

Perit fees are negotiated and typically calculated as a percentage of the construction cost (for new builds) or a fixed fee for permit applications. Fees are not regulated — obtain at least two quotations and agree the scope in writing before instructing.

Common Mistake

Watch out: Proceeding with significant building works in Malta without engaging a perit. Without one, you cannot obtain a PA permit and cannot get a completion certificate.

Expert Tip

Pro tip: Ask your perit specifically whether they have handled applications for properties in your Local Plan zone — zone-specific experience means fewer planning surprises.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is a perit the same as an architect?

In Malta, yes — the perit qualification combines architecture and civil engineering. In some other countries these are separate professions.

Can a foreign architect practise in Malta?

Foreign EU architects can apply for recognition under EU directives, but must register with the Kamra tal-Periti before practising independently.

Who is responsible if the planning application is refused?

The perit is responsible for the technical accuracy of the submission; a refusal based on policy (Local Plan compliance) is a planning risk both parties share.
