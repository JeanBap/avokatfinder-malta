Gozo Property

Gozo is Malta's sister island offering a slower lifestyle, lower property prices, and various stamp duty and residency incentives compared to the Maltese mainland.

Gozo's Property Market

Gozo, Malta's smaller sister island, has developed a distinct property market characterised by traditional Maltese stone farmhouses (dar tar-raba), converted village houses, and modern developments overlooking the Mediterranean. Prices in Gozo are generally 15–30% below equivalent Maltese mainland properties, making it attractive to lifestyle buyers and investors seeking value.

Stamp Duty and Residency Incentives

Malta's government has periodically offered reduced stamp duty rates (as low as 2.5%) on Gozo property to stimulate investment on the island. The Global Residence Programme and Malta Permanent Residence Programme both have lower property thresholds for Gozo (€220,000 and €300,000 respectively vs €275,000 and €375,000 on the mainland). These incentives make Gozo particularly attractive for residency programme applicants.

Traditional Farmhouses (Dar tar-Raba)

Gozo's iconic stone farmhouses — typically a ground-floor agricultural level with a courtyard and upper residential floors — are among the most sought-after properties on the island. Renovating one is a substantial project: building permits must be obtained from the PA's Gozo office, and any works affecting the traditional character require careful compliance with heritage and Local Plan rules. Original stone features must generally be preserved.

AIP Requirements in Gozo

The same AIP permit rules that apply in Malta apply in Gozo — non-EU nationals and EU citizens without 5 years of Maltese residency need an AIP permit for non-SDA properties. Gozo has its own SDA-designated areas where AIP is not required. The Gozo Channel ferry connection and the Ċirkewwa-Mġarr route are the primary links to the Maltese mainland — factor in commute time if you need regular access to Malta.

Rental Market in Gozo

Gozo's rental market is primarily tourism-driven — the island attracts divers, wellness tourists, and villa holiday-makers, particularly from northern Europe. Long-term residential rental demand is lower than Malta. Buyers purchasing for rental yield should model a primarily seasonal occupancy pattern and verify MTA licence requirements for their chosen property type.

Common Mistake

Watch out: Buying a Gozo farmhouse without a structural survey. Traditional stone buildings often have hidden roof, damp, or structural issues that require significant remediation.

Expert Tip

Pro tip: For Gozo farmhouse renovations, engage an architect experienced in PA Gozo heritage applications before purchase — some features (external apertures, stone elevations) have strict preservation conditions that affect your renovation scope.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is the AIP permit easier to get in Gozo?

No — the same rules and process apply as in Malta.

Can I travel to Malta easily from Gozo?

Via the Gozo Channel ferry (~25 min crossing) or helicopter service — but factor in daily commute time if working in Malta.

Is Gozo in the Schengen Area?

Yes — Malta and all its islands are Schengen members.
