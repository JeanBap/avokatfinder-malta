Malta Residency Schemes

Malta offers several residency-by-property programs — including the Global Residence Programme and the Malta Permanent Residence Programme — linking real estate investment to the right to reside in Malta.

Overview of Property-Linked Residency

Malta has established itself as a leading EU residency destination for non-EU nationals, offering multiple residency schemes tied to property investment. These programmes allow qualifying applicants to obtain Maltese residency status — and through it, the right to live, work, and travel freely within the Schengen Area — in exchange for meeting property and financial threshold requirements.

Global Residence Programme (GRP)

The Global Residence Programme targets non-EU nationals who are not ordinarily resident in Malta. To qualify, applicants must either purchase property in Malta for a minimum of €275,000 (€220,000 in Gozo or South Malta) or rent for at least €9,600 per year (€8,750 in Gozo). A minimum income threshold applies (€100,000 annual income or €500,000 in assets), plus a government fee. The GRP grants a special tax status with a flat 15% tax rate on overseas income remitted to Malta.

Malta Permanent Residence Programme (MPRP)

The Malta Permanent Residence Programme offers permanent residency for life to qualifying non-EU nationals. Requirements include purchasing property for a minimum of €375,000 (€300,000 in Gozo) or renting for at least €14,000 per year, plus a non-refundable government contribution of €28,000 (€58,000 if renting). The MPRP grants the right to reside in Malta indefinitely and travel Schengen-free.

Key Conditions

Both programmes require that the property be the applicant's primary Maltese residence throughout the qualifying period, that the applicant hold valid health insurance, and that they not be engaged in employment in Malta (unless separately authorised). The property must be retained for the minimum holding period — typically 5 years — before disposal without affecting residency status.

Due Diligence and Compliance

Residency Malta Agency conducts a four-tier due diligence check on all applicants. Tax compliance, source of funds, and criminal record checks are all part of the process. Properties used for programme purposes must meet the minimum value thresholds and cannot be in certain excluded zones. Using an accredited agent (Approved Agent of Residency Malta) is advisable to navigate the application correctly.

Common Mistake

Watch out: Using a property that has not yet obtained final title (e.g. a property under emphyteusis with complex title) for a residency programme application. Clear freehold title is strongly preferred.

Expert Tip

Pro tip: Engage an accredited Approved Agent from day one — programme rules change frequently and an accredited agent has the most current interpretation of threshold and compliance requirements.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I get Maltese citizenship through property investment?

Residency programmes give residency, not citizenship. The Malta Citizenship by Direct Investment programme is separate and has stricter requirements.

Does my family qualify too?

Yes — spouse and dependent children can be included in most residency applications as dependants.

Can I work in Malta under the GRP?

Generally no — the GRP is for passive residents. Employment requires a separate work permit.
