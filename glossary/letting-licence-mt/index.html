Letting Licence Malta

A letting licence from the Malta Tourism Authority (MTA) is required before any short-term holiday let can be legally operated in Malta, with separate categories for tourist accommodation.

Why a Licence Is Required

Malta regulates short-term tourist accommodation under the Travel and Tourism Services Act. Any property offered for rent to tourists for periods of less than 3 months must be licensed by the Malta Tourism Authority (MTA). Operating without a licence exposes the owner to fines and potential forced closure. The MTA classifies tourist accommodation into several categories with different standards and inspection requirements.

Categories Relevant to Residential Property

For residential property owners wishing to let via Airbnb or similar platforms, the relevant MTA category is typically Tourist Furnished Apartment (for self-catering units) or Tourist Villa/Farmhouse (for standalone properties). Each category has minimum space, furnishing, safety equipment (smoke detectors, fire extinguishers), and accessibility standards. The property must meet these standards before an inspection is passed and a licence granted.

Application Process

The MTA application includes: completed application form, site plan, title/lease documentation proving the applicant's right to offer the property, planning permit for the building, and payment of the licence fee. An MTA inspector visits the property to verify compliance with standards before the licence is issued. Licences are renewable annually and must be displayed in the property.

Platform Compliance

Airbnb, Booking.com, and similar platforms operating in Malta are required by law to verify that properties listed have a valid MTA licence number. Unlicensed listings may be removed by the platform and the operator faces enforcement from the MTA. Include the MTA licence number in all online listings.

Condominium Restrictions

Even with an MTA licence, operating a short-term let in a condominium building may be prohibited by the condominium's deed of conditions (deed of servitude). Check the condominium deed before investing in a property specifically for tourist letting — the MTA cannot override private property restrictions.

Common Mistake

Watch out: Listing a property on Airbnb without an MTA licence. Fines are issued and platforms are now required to report or remove unlicensed listings.

Expert Tip

Pro tip: Apply for the MTA licence before you complete your purchase — the processing time (typically 4–8 weeks) and any remedial works needed to pass inspection should be factored into your investment timeline.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I let a property long-term without an MTA licence?

Yes — the MTA licence applies to short-term tourist rentals only. Long-term residential tenancies do not require it.

Is the licence transferable if I sell the property?

No — the licence is personal to the operator. A new owner must apply for their own licence.

What standards does the MTA require?

Minimum room sizes, smoke alarms, fire extinguishers, first aid kit, and specific furnishing standards — the full checklist is on the MTA website.
