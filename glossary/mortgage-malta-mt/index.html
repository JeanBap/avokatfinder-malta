Mortgage Malta

A Maltese mortgage (ipoteka) is a registered security interest over real property granted to a bank in exchange for a home loan, governed by the Civil Code and subject to local LTV and income assessment rules.

How Maltese Mortgages Work

In Malta, a property purchase loan is secured by a ipoteka (hypothec/mortgage) registered at the Public Registry. The bank lends against the value of the property and, in the event of default, has priority right to recover from the sale proceeds. Mortgages are the standard financing mechanism for Maltese and foreign buyers alike, though non-residents face tighter LTV limits.

Loan-to-Value (LTV) Rules

Maltese banks typically lend up to 90% LTV for Maltese residents buying their primary residence, 80% for Maltese residents buying secondary or investment property, and 70–75% for non-resident buyers. These limits are set by the Malta Financial Services Authority's macroprudential rules and can change — verify the current limits with your bank at the outset.

Debt-Service Ratio

Banks also apply a debt-to-income (DTI) test: total monthly debt servicing (all loans including the new mortgage) generally cannot exceed 40% of gross income. For non-residents whose income is earned abroad, banks typically require audited accounts or tax returns and may apply a haircut to the declared income figure. Self-employed applicants often face more conservative income assessments.

Bank Guarantee vs Hypothec

Some Maltese banks offer a bank guarantee as an alternative to a full hypothec for smaller loans or bridging finance. A bank guarantee is not a registered real security interest — it is a personal undertaking. For standard purchase mortgages, a registered hypothec is the norm and provides the bank with the strongest security position.

Process and Timeline

A Maltese mortgage application typically takes 4–8 weeks from submission to formal offer. The bank commissions an independent valuation of the property (cost borne by the applicant) and a credit check. The formal mortgage offer includes the interest rate, term, monthly repayment, and conditions. Once accepted, the mortgage deed is signed at the same time as or just before the property deed of sale, with the bank disbursing directly to the seller's account.

Common Mistake

Watch out: Relying on a verbal indication of mortgage approval to sign the promise of sale. Only a formal written mortgage offer should be treated as confirmed — verbal pre-approvals are not binding.

Expert Tip

Pro tip: If buying as a non-resident, approach Maltese banks well before the promise of sale — some banks require physical branch visits or certified document translations that add weeks to the process.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I get a Maltese mortgage in a foreign currency?

Some Maltese banks offer EUR/USD mortgages for non-residents; currency risk can be significant if your income is in a different currency.

Is mortgage interest tax deductible in Malta?

For owner-occupied primary residences, interest relief is available subject to caps — verify with a Maltese tax adviser.

Can the mortgage be taken over by the buyer if I sell?

In some cases, banks allow mortgage assumption — but this requires full bank approval and re-underwriting of the incoming buyer.
