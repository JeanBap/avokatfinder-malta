Ground Rent Malta

Ground rent in Malta (cens) is the periodic payment made by the holder of an emphyteutical title to the landowner, forming an ongoing obligation that runs with the property.

What Is Ground Rent in Malta?

The cens — commonly called ground rent — is the periodic payment made by the emphyteuta (the person holding emphyteutical rights over land) to the dominus (the landowner or the entity entitled to receive the ground rent). Historically set in Malta at low nominal amounts, cens obligations are still attached to thousands of properties, particularly in older urban areas and government-granted plots.

Government Ground Rents

A significant portion of Maltese ground rents are owed to the Government of Malta, arising from the historical granting of emphyteutical rights to private parties. The Government periodically offers redemption schemes allowing emphyteutas to pay a lump sum and extinguish the ground rent forever, converting their title to full freehold (propjeta' libera). These redemption exercises are often undersubscribed despite being financially advantageous.

Private Ground Rents

Private ground rents — owed to former landowners or their heirs — are more complex. The obligation passes with the land to every successive buyer. Finding and paying the person entitled to receive a private ground rent can become difficult over generations, as the entitled party may have died, emigrated, or subdivided their entitlement among heirs. Unpaid private ground rents can theoretically give rise to legal action by the dominus.

Impact on Mortgages and Sales

When a property with a ground rent is sold, the notary should disclose the ground rent obligation in the deed. Maltese banks generally lend against emphyteutical properties with ground rents, but the ground rent must be disclosed and the bank must be satisfied about the remaining term (for temporary emphyteusis). Ground rents are sometimes capitalised and deducted from the sale price at the deed stage.

Checking Ground Rent Status

The original emphyteutical deed will specify the cens amount, due dates, and any conditions. The Public Registry holds records of emphyteutical grants. Before buying an older Maltese property, confirm whether a ground rent applies, to whom it is owed, whether it is current, and whether redemption is possible and at what cost.

Common Mistake

Watch out: Buying an old Malta town house without asking whether a ground rent exists. Discovering a private cens after purchase means an obligation to a party you may struggle to identify.

Expert Tip

Pro tip: If a government ground rent applies, apply for redemption immediately after purchase while the process is straightforward — private ground rents become progressively harder to manage as generations of heirs accumulate.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much is a typical cens in Malta?

Nominal amounts set decades ago — sometimes as low as €5–€20 per year. But the obligation to pay is perpetual unless redeemed.

What happens if I don't pay the cens?

The dominus can bring legal action. For government cens, arrears accumulate with interest. Persistent non-payment could in theory give rise to action against the title.

How do I find out who receives my cens?

Check the original emphyteutical deed at the Public Registry — your notary can trace the entitled party.
